Part 135 Unscheduled Operations

Regulatory reference: 14 CFR §135.267 — Flight time limitations and rest requirements: Unscheduled one- and two-pilot crews. For reference and record-keeping only. Always verify compliance with your OpSpec, POI, and company manual. All times should be consistently Zulu or local — pick one.

§135.267 Quick Reference

Rolling 24-Hour Flight Time

Single pilot: max 8 hours in any consecutive 24-hour window.
Dual pilot: max 10 hours.
Window ends at the On Blocks time of the current leg.

10-Hour Look-Back Rest

The pilot must have received at least 10 consecutive hours of rest in the 24 hours preceding the completion of any flight segment.

14-Hour Duty Day

Total duty period (Show Time to Release Time) must not exceed 14 hours.

Quarterly Rest

Each pilot must receive at least 13 24-hour rest periods per calendar quarter. Mark rest days using the checkbox in the Add Entry form.

Exceedance Rest Multiplier

If flight time is exceeded (weather/delays):
<30 min over → 11 hrs rest
30–60 min over → 12 hrs rest
>60 min over → 16 hrs rest

How to Use

One entry per flight leg. For multi-leg days, use Add Another Leg. Show/Release Time and Rest are shared across all legs in a duty period. Mark Part 91 legs to log them without affecting §135 limits.
